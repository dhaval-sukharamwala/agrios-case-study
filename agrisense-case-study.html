Overview

A control room for the farm.

Agriculture powers a huge share of India’s economy — yet many farmers still steer critical decisions with fragmented information, manual checks, and intuition. Weather swings, volatile market prices, pest outbreaks, and slow access to insight quietly erode both yield and income.

AgriSense is a concept for an AI-powered farm management platform that pulls operations, environment, and finance into a single intelligent dashboard — so farmers, cooperatives, and agribusinesses can monitor, predict, and optimize in one place.

It doesn’t just show numbers. It surfaces what matters now, the risk behind it, and the next action to take.

The Problem

Every answer lives in a different place.

A farmer checks weather in one app, mandi prices from a local trader, soil notes on paper, irrigation by memory, and scheme eligibility by word of mouth. Nothing talks to anything.

Design Principles

Four rules that governed every screen.

01

Simplicity

Large volumes of agricultural data should feel approachable — never like a spreadsheet a farmer has to decode.

02

Prioritization

Critical alerts always appear before historical analytics. Urgency, not chronology, drives the layout.

03

Actionability

Every insight ends in a recommended next step — schedule, delay, scout, hold, or sell.

04

Accessibility

Built for varied digital literacy: clear icons, strong contrast, and intuitive navigation throughout.

Reflection
What designing for agriculture taught me.

Designing for agriculture goes far beyond building dashboards. It means translating complex environmental, operational, and financial data into clear, timely actions a farmer can trust and take.

The work reinforced how much prioritization matters — deciding what a user sees first is a design decision with real consequences. It pushed me to simplify dense workflows, and to design deliberately for users across a wide spectrum of digital literacy.

Good agri-design isn’t about showing more data. It’s about showing the right thing, at the right moment, with the next step already in view.
